## Florida-Specific Risks & Notes

- **Hurricane insurance:** $8,350/yr (1% of price) is in line with PBC reality of $5K–$8K/yr for a $500K home, scaled up. Roof age >15 yrs would push 25–50% higher, easily $10K–$12K. South FL saw a 11.9% PBC decrease in 2026, so trend is favorable, but a single named storm losing roof coverage flips this. **Get a wind mitigation inspection and current roof age before underwriting.**
- **Flood zone:** Wellington is inland but contains Zone X and AE pockets — Moon Bay Cir is generally low-risk, but pull the elevation cert. Flood policy not modeled.
- **HOA risk:** Olympia HOA $300–$500/mo plus master assessments; modeled at $400. Verify sub-village dues — some run higher.
- **Florida no state income tax:** Boosts after-tax return vs. similar property in CA/NY/IL — partially offsets the negative cash flow on an after-tax basis.
- **Equestrian/Wellington seasonal dynamics:** Nov–Mar polo/WEF season inflates short-term rents 80–120%. A hybrid annual-base + seasonal-premium strategy is plausible (e.g., furnish for Jan–Mar Wellington Equestrian Festival), but operationally complex and reduces tenant stability.
- **Property tax reset on sale:** Florida's "Save Our Homes" 3% cap resets at purchase. The $5,538 historical tax figure is the prior owner's homesteaded basis — an investor will see assessment jump to market and pay the full ~1.2%. Already modeled.
- **Insurance carrier risk:** Citizens Property Insurance is the FL state-backed insurer of last resort. Many private carriers have exited PBC. Confirm a binding quote pre-close.
