## Market Classification

**Soft Buyer's Market — leaning balanced for well-priced inventory**

Reasoning:
- ~8 months of supply (>6 months = buyer's market by NAR convention)
- Active inventory up 200%+ since 2024 trough
- Sale-to-list ratio at ~94% means buyers consistently negotiating 5-6% off original ask
- Listing prices dropping 11% YoY while sale prices held +2.8% to +8.3% YoY = sellers are coming down to meet buyers, not the other way around
- BUT: well-priced, well-condition homes are moving in <30 days, indicating demand still exists at the right number

Bottom line: Buyers have leverage; sellers must price right and present well. This is NOT a distressed market — it's a recalibration after the 2021-2023 frenzy.

## Wellington Seasonality (the WEF effect)

Wellington's housing market has **the most pronounced seasonal pattern in South Florida** because of the Winter Equestrian Festival (WEF) at Wellington International.

**Jan-Apr (WEF Season):**
- WEF runs late December through April
- Average competitor/team stay: ~67 days (per Palm Beach County Sports Commission)
- 2023 economic impact: $352M to PBC
- Seasonal rentals: $20,000-$100,000/month (yes, per month) for equestrian-friendly homes
- Buyer activity peaks: snowbirds + equestrian families house-hunt while in town
- Sale prices firm; DOM compresses

**May-Oct (Off-season):**
- Buyer flow collapses
- Inventory builds
- Hurricane season insurance/closing friction
- Best buy-side leverage window

**Nov-Dec:**
- Pre-season ramp; strategic listing window for high-end sellers

**Implication for valuation:** A May 2026 market read understates demand for equestrian-zoned/WEF-proximate homes. List timing matters enormously — Q4 2026 list is materially better than Q3 for prestige inventory.

## Distressed / Foreclosure Activity

Direct 33414-specific foreclosure counts were not surfaced, but the regional picture:

- **Palm Beach County:** Foreclosure inventory concentrated in Boynton Beach, West Palm Beach, and western suburban corridors — NOT historically Wellington. Wellington has a higher-equity owner profile (equestrian-second-home + established families) which suppresses NOD/REO flow.
- **Florida-wide trend (2026):** Insurance-driven distress is rising — owners surrendering homes they can't afford to insure. This is creating a NEW pipeline of motivated sellers (not always full foreclosures — often "must sell" listings).
- **Cash sales surging in FL** specifically driven by insurance crisis — Homeinc and others report 2026 spike in cash transactions as financed buyers can't clear insurance hurdles.
- **Auction activity:** Palm Beach County Clerk runs online foreclosure auctions M-Th 10am; Bid4Assets handles sheriff sales.

**DealGapIQ angle:** Wellington 33414 is not a foreclosure-volume play. It's a price-discovery/motivated-seller play in a softening market with stretched insurance dynamics. The "deal gap" here is between asking prices anchored to 2022 peaks and clearing prices that reflect 2026 buyer reality + insurance carry costs.

## Risks & Tailwinds

### Headwinds
- **FL insurance:** Avg PBC premium $5,000-$8,000/yr (coastal higher). 2026 brought first meaningful relief — Citizens -8.7%, PBC -11.9% — but premiums still 2-3x 2019 levels. Material drag on affordability and net rental yield.
- **Hurricane risk premium:** Persistent. Wellington is inland (~12 mi from coast) so flood/wind exposure is lower than Boca/Palm Beach proper, but still in Cat-3+ wind zone. Western Wellington has C-zone flood concerns.
- **Inventory overhang:** 8 months of supply will pressure prices through summer 2026 if rates stay elevated.
- **Equestrian economy concentration:** WEF crowd is global-rich and rate-insensitive, but if international buyer flow weakens (FX, tax, geopolitics), top of market is exposed.
- **Rate sensitivity:** Median price points ($655K-$880K) are squarely in the rate-shock zone for financed buyers.

### Tailwinds
- **NY/NJ/CA migration:** Still net positive into PBC; Wellington benefits as the "family-friendly equestrian alternative" to coastal Palm Beach
- **WEF franchise:** Wellington International continues investing in venue; equestrian economy is structurally embedded
- **Insurance stabilization:** First downward print in years — improving carry math marginally
- **No state income tax:** Continues to be the structural FL pull factor for high earners
- **Below-average 2026 hurricane season forecast** (CSU): Could compress risk premium through Q4
- **School quality:** Wellington schools (Equestrian Trails Elem, Wellington HS) remain a draw
