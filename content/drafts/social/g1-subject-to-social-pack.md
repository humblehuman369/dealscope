>

>

**Tweet 3:**
> Why $603/month matters more than $70K extra cash:
>
> The Sub2 loan stays in the seller's name. It doesn't count against your DTI. It doesn't show up against your conventional 10-loan limit.
>
> You can buy your 11th house this way after the bank cut you off at 10.

**Tweet 4:**
> Why a seller says yes:
>
> 1. They're at zero equity and a standard sale won't net them anything after commissions
> 2. Property is hard to finance conventionally (deferred maintenance, HOA in litigation)
> 3. They want a fast, certain close — 14 days, no contingencies

**Tweet 5:**
> The risks nobody mentions in a YouTube video:
>
> 1. Due-on-Sale clause (the lender's option, rarely exercised — but "rarely" isn't "never")
> 2. Insurance has to be rewritten correctly or claims don't pay
> 3. Seller has to forward escrow refunds and tax notices for 5–20 years

**Tweet 6:**
> Sub2 fits when:
> ✓ Seller's existing rate is well below market
> ✓ Seller has low/zero equity
> ✓ Property cash-flows at the existing payment but not at today's
> ✓ You're past the 10-loan conventional limit
>
> Sub2 doesn't fit when any of those flips.
