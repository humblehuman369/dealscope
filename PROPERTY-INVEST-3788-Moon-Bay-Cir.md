## Risks Across All Strategies

1. **Insurance.** Palm Beach County 2026 homeowners avg $5K–$8K, declining ~12% YoY but still highest in the U.S. STR insurance pushes higher. One bad hurricane season reverses the trend overnight. Underwrite at 1.25%–1.5% of value, not 1%, for stress test.
2. **Hurricane / wind / flood.** Wellington is inland (10+ mi from coast) so storm surge is low, but wind and inland flood risk remain. Roof age/4-point inspection critical for insurability.
3. **HOA STR restrictions.** Olympia is a guard-gated HOA — verify in writing whether short-term rentals are permitted, and if so, the minimum lease term. Many South Florida HOAs cap at 3 or 6-month minimums (which actually *fits* the WEF seasonal-lease model well, but kills nightly Airbnb).
4. **Equestrian-economy concentration.** Wellington's premium pricing rests on WEF and the polo/horse community. A move of WEF (Wellington International redevelopment debates have been ongoing), tax-policy changes in NY/NJ that reduce snowbird flow, or a recession in luxury discretionary spending all compress seasonal rents.
5. **Rate / exit risk.** Today's 6% mortgage rate is the underwrite. Rate moves up = price compression. Selling out in years 1–2 with high closing/holding costs is a loser unless market keeps appreciating.
6. **Florida insurance carrier exits.** Citizens (state-backed) is increasingly the only option in many ZIPs. Watch for non-renewal events.
7. **Property tax reset on sale.** Florida's Save-Our-Homes cap protects current owners; a new buyer gets reassessed at market — taxes will jump materially from prior owner's base.
8. **Appreciation volatility.** 2025 saw a brief -5% YoY blip in 33414 before April 2026 +8% rebound. Florida luxury markets are choppy.
