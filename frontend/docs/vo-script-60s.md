# DealGapIQ — 60-Second Explainer Video
## Voiceover Script (v1)

**Length:** 60 seconds
**Word count:** ~125 words (~125 wpm — relaxed conversational pace, leaves room for visuals to breathe)
**Format:** Single-talent VO, screen recording underneath
**Tone:** Confident, warm, founder-energy — not radio announcer

---

## Direction for the talent

> Read like you're explaining your own product to a friend over coffee — not pitching from a stage. Slight smile in the voice. Trust comes from steadiness, not energy.
>
> Pace: 125 wpm. Leave a half-beat after every sentence. The visuals do half the work.
>
> Avoid: announcer cadence, upward inflections at the end of statements, breathy whispering, fake urgency.
>
> Reference vibe: a TechCrunch founder demo voiceover. Marques Brownlee narrating his own product video. Not a Super Bowl ad.

## Pronunciation key

| Word | Say it like |
| --- | --- |
| DealGapIQ | "Deal-Gap-EYE-Q" (each letter, not "ick") |
| BRRRR | "B-R-R-R" — say each letter, drag the last two slightly: "B, R, R, R" |
| FHA / VA | "F-H-A" / "V-A" (letter by letter, never "fuh" or "vah") |
| Proforma | "pro-FOR-muh" |
| Off-market | "OFF-market" (slight emphasis on "off") |
| ZIP | rhymes with "tip" — single syllable, not "zip code" |

## Pause notation

- `/` = short breath (~0.3s)
- `//` = longer pause between sections (~0.6s)
- **Bold** = emphasized word
- *Italic* = soften / drop in volume slightly

## The script (read top to bottom)

### [0:00 – 0:05] — Cold open
*B-roll: driving past a house, hand reaches for phone*

> You drive past a house. /
> You wonder what it'd be worth as a rental. //
> **Now you know.** *In fifteen seconds.*

*(20 words / 5s = 240 wpm with pauses — feels brisk because of the hook energy)*

---

### [0:05 – 0:12] — Scan
*B-roll: phone pointed at house, AR cone overlay, "Property Found" appears*

> **Point.** /
> **Scan.** /
> **Done.**

*(3 words / 7s — deliberately slow. Each word is its own beat. Let the visual land.)*

---

### [0:12 – 0:23] — Verdict
*B-roll: cut to Verdict page, three values pan in, Deal Gap bar animates*

> Target Buy. /
> Income Value. /
> Market Price. //
> The **Deal Gap** between them tells you instantly: /
> *walk away,* /
> *negotiate,* /
> or **write the offer.**

*(28 words / 11s = 153 wpm — the hero moment, pace tightens)*

---

### [0:23 – 0:32] — Strategy
*B-roll: strategy pills tap through, numbers update for each*

> Same property — / **six** ways to profit. //
> Long-term rental. / B-R-R-R. / Flip. / House hack. //
> Whatever **fits your strategy.**

*(19 words / 9s = 127 wpm)*

---

### [0:32 – 0:42] — Appraiser + Estimator
*B-roll: comp map with pins, rehab estimator presets, Excel proforma export*

> Live comps. /
> Local rehab pricing. /
> Excel-ready proformas. //
> The whole investor workbench — / **built for one tap.**

*(17 words / 10s = 102 wpm — slower, lets visuals carry the breadth)*

---

### [0:42 – 0:52] — Map Search 🆕
*B-roll: satellite view with green/yellow/red pins, foreclosure filter chip activates, pin opens to verdict*

> Don't have an address yet? //
> Hunt deals across an **entire ZIP.** /
> Foreclosures. / Pre-foreclosures. / Auctions. //
> Every parcel — *pre-graded.*

*(19 words / 10s = 114 wpm)*

---

### [0:52 – 0:60] — Close
*B-roll: DealGapIQ logo, app store badges, URL on-screen*

> **DealGapIQ.** //
> Not a listing site. /
> A **deal decision engine.**

*(11 words / 8s = 82 wpm — final tag, pace stretches, conviction lands)*

> *On-screen text (not VO):*
> 3 free scans / month · No credit card · DealGapIQ.com

## Audio deliverables to request from the talent

1. **Master read** — full 60-second performance, single take preferred
2. **Per-section stems** — 7 individual clips (one per scene above), so the editor can re-time without re-recording
3. **Two alternate reads** of the close ("DealGapIQ. Not a listing site. A deal decision engine.") — one slightly warmer, one slightly more declarative. Pick the better one in editing.
4. **WAV format**, 48 kHz / 24-bit minimum
5. **No noise reduction or EQ applied** — let your editor handle post

---

## When you record your own version (later)

Use this exact script unchanged. Same pause marks, same emphasis. The only thing that changes is the voice — which is the only variable you're testing. Record:

1. **Three full takes** of the master read, back to back, in one session
2. Each section as a separate file too (matches the talent's stems)
3. Same room, same time of day, same mic distance — keep everything constant except the voice itself

That gives you a clean A/B: same words, same pacing, same visuals — just **founder voice vs. professional voice**. Drop both edits side-by-side and you'll know within an hour which one feels right. Or run both as paid social ads at $50/each and let CTR decide.
