# What Is NOI (Net Operating Income)? The Foundation of Every Rental Analysis

NOI is the dollar amount of annual income a rental property produces *after* operating expenses but *before* mortgage payments and income taxes. Every other rental metric — cap rate, DSCR, cash-on-cash return — starts here.

## The 30-second definition

```
NOI = Effective Gross Income − Operating Expenses
```

Where:

- **Effective Gross Income (EGI)** = annual rent × (1 − vacancy_rate) + other income
- **Operating expenses** = property taxes + insurance + maintenance + property management + utilities + landscaping + pest control + HOA fees

NOI explicitly excludes the mortgage payment, depreciation, and capital improvements. That's intentional — NOI describes the *property*, not the financing or tax strategy of the owner.

## A real-numbers example

A duplex rents for $3,200/month with 5% vacancy assumed.

- Gross annual rent: $3,200 × 12 = $38,400
- Vacancy loss: $38,400 × 5% = $1,920
- **Effective Gross Income: $36,480**

Annual operating expenses:

- Property taxes: $4,800
- Insurance: $1,800
- Maintenance (5% of EGI): $1,824
- Property management: $0 (self-managed)
- HOA: $0
- **Total operating expenses: $8,424**

**NOI = $36,480 − $8,424 = $28,056**

That $28,056 is what the property earns. From there, the mortgage payment determines cash flow, and the cap rate is `$28,056 / purchase_price`.

## What is NOT an operating expense

A common error: investors include the mortgage payment in operating expenses. Don't. The mortgage is debt service, not an operating cost. Excluding it is what makes NOI comparable across properties with different financing.

Also excluded:

- Capital expenditures (a new roof, HVAC replacement) — these are capitalized, not expensed
- Depreciation — a tax concept, not a real cash outflow
- Income taxes — depends on the owner's tax situation, not the property
- Improvements that increase property value — capitalized

## What feeds NOI vs what feeds cash flow

| Metric | Formula | What it measures |
|--------|---------|------------------|
| NOI | EGI − OpEx | Property's earning power |
| Annual cash flow | NOI − annual debt service | Owner's pocket cash |
| Cap rate | NOI / purchase price | Unleveraged yield |
| DSCR | NOI / annual debt service | Lender's safety margin |
| Cash-on-cash | annual cash flow / cash invested | Leveraged return |

## FAQ

**Should NOI include capital improvements?**
No. Capital improvements (roof, HVAC, additions) are capitalized and depreciated — they don't hit NOI. Some investors include a "reserve for capex" line as an operating expense (typically 5–10% of EGI). DealGapIQ uses 5% maintenance default; capex reserves are a separate user-configurable line.

**Does NOI include property management I do myself?**
Best practice: yes, even if you self-manage. Charging market rate for management (typically 8–10% of gross rent) makes the analysis comparable to professionally managed properties and gives you a realistic picture if you ever hand it off.

**What's a good NOI?**
There's no absolute answer — NOI is a dollar amount. What matters is NOI *relative to purchase price* (cap rate) and NOI *relative to debt service* (DSCR).
