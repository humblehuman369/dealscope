# App Store Subtitle (30 character limit)

App Store Connect → My Apps → DealGapIQ → App Information → **Subtitle**

The subtitle appears under the app name in search results, on the product page, and in the Today / Apps / Games tabs. It is the **second-most-prominent text** after the app name, often visible without tapping into the listing.

---

## Recommended (PRIMARY)

> **Every US deal, pre-scored.** _(26 chars — leaves 4 chars headroom for future tweaks)_

**Why this wins**
- "Every" + "US" quantifies the scope without using a vague claim like "all properties"
- "Pre-scored" is the unique mechanic — no competitor on the App Store does this
- Reads as a complete, declarative sentence in 4 words — scannable in under 1 second
- Reinforces the same "pre-scored for profit" language used in the hero screenshot subhead, creating cohesion across the listing
- Periodic period adds a confident, definitive tone

## Tested but rejected

| Subtitle | Chars | Why rejected |
|---|---|---|
| `Every listing scored for profit.` | 32 | Over the 30-char limit |
| `Hunt deals through investor's lens.` | 35 | Over the limit; the hero screenshot already carries this |
| `Real estate deal scoring app` | 28 | Generic; sounds like it could be any tool |
| `Find investment properties fast` | 31 | Generic; doesn't differentiate from Zillow / Realtor.com |
| `AI-powered property analysis` | 28 | "AI-powered" is a saturated claim that no longer earns trust |

## A/B test plan

After launch, test the **PRIMARY against #2 (Discovery-driven)** and **#3 (DEAL/MAYBE/PASS)** in 30-day cycles. Apple lets you change the subtitle on every app update, so cycle through with each release. Track install conversion rate (App Store Connect → Analytics → App Store → Conversion Rate) and pick the winner.
