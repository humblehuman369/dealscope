# Neighborhood Analysis — 3788 Moon Bay Cir, Wellington FL 33414

## Community / HOA

**Community:** Grand Isles at Wellington
**Type:** Guard-gated single-family home community (24/7 manned security gate + secondary residents-only gate)
**Builder/Vintage:** Built 1997–2000 by Centex Homes and Lennar
**Size:** 611 homes across five villages — Carmel, Harbor Cove, Emerald Bay, Emerald Pointe, and Sausalito
**Location:** North side of Lake Worth Road, just west of SR-7 (US-441)
**HOA fee:** ~$200–$220/month (one of the lowest maintenance fees in Wellington)
**HOA includes:** Bulk Comcast cable, full-time on-site property manager, 24/7 manned security
**Age restriction:** None (all-ages, family-oriented)

**Amenities (HOA-included):**
- Renovated lakefront clubhouse with party room
- Heated swimming pool + kiddie pool
- Fully equipped fitness center
- Lighted tennis / pickleball courts
- Lighted basketball court
- Tot lot / playground
- Walking, jogging and biking paths
- Many homes have lake or preserve views

**Why it matters:** Grand Isles is a "value-tier" gated community in Wellington — buyers get manned security, a full amenity package, and equestrian-corridor proximity at HOA dues that run ~half what comparable Wellington gated communities charge.

## Crime & Safety

- **Wellington overall crime rate:** ~924.5 per 100,000 (FBI), roughly **56% below the national average**
- **Violent crime:** ~1.32 per 1,000 residents — well below national rate (4.82 nat'l avg)
- **Property crime:** ~10.87 per 1,000 — also below national average
- **Rankings:** SafeWise — 13th safest city in Florida; #37 of 160 FL cities by CrimeGrade; #4,104 of 9,896 US cities
- **Resident sentiment:** ~73% report feeling safe walking at night

**Within Grand Isles:** A 24/7 manned guard gate and secondary residents-only gate further reduce property-crime exposure beyond the already-low Wellington baseline. Crime risk inside the community is materially lower than the city average.

**Concern:** No Wellington-specific crime trend reversal in 2025 data, but Palm Beach County unemployment ticked up from 3.5% to 5.0% Nov 2024 → Nov 2025 — historically a leading indicator for property-crime upticks. Worth watching, not yet a flag.

## Walkability & Transit

- **Walk Score (zip 33414 average):** ~19 (Car-Dependent)
- **Estimated Walk Score for Moon Bay Cir specifically:** **~7–10** (Grand Isles sits in an interior residential pocket; nearest commercial is Lake Worth Rd / SR-7 corridor)
- **Bike Score:** ~28–29 (Wellington has expanding bike paths but not commuter-grade infrastructure)
- **Transit Score:** **0** (effectively no public transit; Palm Tran service is limited)

**Day-to-day feasibility:** Car-required for essentially all errands. Lake Worth Rd corridor (Publix, Walgreens, restaurants) is ~5 min by car; Mall at Wellington Green ~10 min; Whole Foods, Trader Joe's, and the Wellington Green commercial cluster all <15 min. Within Grand Isles itself, the internal walking/biking/jogging paths are pleasant and safe — walkability is "lifestyle" inside the gates, not "errand" outside them.

## Amenities & Lifestyle

**Equestrian (Wellington's signature):**
- **Wellington International** (formerly PBIEC) — 111-acre venue, 18 arenas, 400 stalls; hosts the **Winter Equestrian Festival** Dec 31, 2025 – Mar 29, 2026 (13 weeks, **$16M record prize money**) — ~10 min from Moon Bay Cir
- **International Polo Club / National Polo Center** — ~3 min away; one of the largest polo facilities in the US
- **Global Dressage Festival** during winter season
- Wellington equestrian sports drive an estimated **$600M annual economic impact** in Palm Beach County

**Healthcare:**
- **Wellington Regional Medical Center** (235-bed acute care, since 1986) at 10101 Forest Hill Blvd — ~5–10 min away

**Shopping & Dining:**
- **Mall at Wellington Green** — anchor regional mall
- **Whole Foods, Trader Joe's, Publix** — all within ~10 min
- Forest Hill / Lake Worth / SR-7 corridor — full restaurant range

**Recreation:**
- **Village Park Athletics Complex**, Wellington Sports Academy
- Multiple public/private golf courses (Binks Forest, Wanderers, Wellington National, Palm Beach Polo & CC)
- 80+ miles of bridle paths and bike trails throughout Wellington

**Connectivity:** ~5 min to Florida's Turnpike; ~30 min to PBI Airport; ~25 min to Palm Beach beaches.

## Growth & Future

- **Wellington population growth:** modest (~0.66%/yr) — "built-out" suburb with limited large-tract greenfield. Growth is incremental and targeted.
- **Palm Beach County:** projected +4% population by 2026; GDP >$131B (would rank ~63rd largest economy globally)
- **Job market:** West Palm Beach-Boca Raton-Delray Beach metro added ~9,600 jobs (+1.4%) YoY; Education/Health Services +6.6% — fastest-growing sector
- **Equestrian capital investment:** Wellington International announced "transformative upgrade" alongside the record $16M 2026 WEF purse — sustained capital flow signals long-term equestrian-tourism demand
- **Development tension:** Wellington voters have flagged "development pressure" as a 2024–2026 political issue; expect zoning to remain protective of the equestrian preserve, which insulates existing home values from oversupply
- **Headwind:** Florida insurance + property-tax pressure remains a statewide friction on net carry

**Trajectory:** Mature, slow-growing, high-income, supply-constrained — the classic profile of price stability with appreciation tied to equestrian-tourism demand and South Florida in-migration rather than local jobs.

## Buyer Fit

**Best-fit buyers (in priority order):**

1. **Equestrian-adjacent families** — parents of young riders, WEF-season participants who want a primary or seasonal home walking-distance-by-car to Wellington International and the Polo Club without paying $2M+ to be on a horse-zoned lot.
2. **Move-up Wellington families with elementary-age kids** — the Equestrian Trails 10/10 rating is genuinely rare and is a multi-year price tailwind for homes in this attendance zone.
3. **Snowbirds and seasonal renters** — proximity to WEF (Dec–Mar) creates a real seasonal-rental thesis. WEF tenants will pay premium for safe, gated, family-friendly homes within 10 min of the showgrounds; off-season the manned gate and amenities still appeal to snowbirds.
4. **Investors targeting WEF seasonal rentals** — 13-week WEF + early polo season = a plausible 4–5 month premium-rental window. The catch: HOA / Wellington short-term rental rules need to be verified before underwriting (Wellington has tightened STR rules in recent years; many gated HOAs prohibit < 6-month leases).
5. **Buy-and-hold investors on a long-term thesis** — supply-constrained, equestrian-tourism-anchored, A-rated elementary. Lower yield, higher stability play.

**Poor fit:**
- Walk-everywhere urban lifestyle buyers
- Buyers prioritizing GreatSchools-rated high school over middle (consider a charter/magnet boundary if HS rating is decisive)
- Pure cash-flow investors (entry prices and HOA + insurance + tax stack make 1% rule unlikely)
