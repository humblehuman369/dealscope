## 1. How to Use This Playbook

**Who this is for:** Marketing agencies, freelance copywriters, social media
managers, PR contacts, podcast bookers, and anyone building campaigns for
DealGapIQ without access to internal systems.

**What to do with it:**
- Start with §2 (briefing) and §4 (messaging architecture) before writing anything.
- Pull ready-to-use copy from §5 — do not rewrite from scratch unless the channel
  requires it.
- Match voice rules in §3 on every asset.
- Use §9–§10 for campaign structure and channel cadence.
- Check §15 before publishing — compliance is non-negotiable.

**Golden rules:**
1. Lead with **"Hunt. Score. Close."** on human-first surfaces (ads, hero, social).
2. Always use the **speed number:** **15 seconds** — not "instant," not "in minutes."
3. Every asset should hit at least **one act** (Hunt, Score, or Close). Best assets hit all three.
4. Close with the **manifesto** when you need an emotional beat: *"The price tag isn't the deal. The structure is."*
5. Ask: *Does this sound like a calculator, or a platform that finds the deal, scores it, and closes it?* If the first — rewrite.

## 2. 60-Second Briefing

### What DealGapIQ is

DealGapIQ is a real-estate investment platform that takes any US property address
and returns a **15-second verdict** — backed by multi-source valuation — plus
**four pre-built ways to structure the deal** and the **negotiation script** for
each. Web, iOS, and Android. Free to start.

### The problem

Listing sites end at price. Investor calculators end at cash flow. Neither tells
an investor *how to actually close a marginal deal*. Beginner investors see a
negative Deal Gap and assume there are no deals — when really, the deal just
needs to be structured.

### The Deal Gap (proprietary differentiator)

The **Deal Gap** is the percentage distance between the asking price and the
price where the deal actually works for the investor.

- **Positive Deal Gap** — priced below target; built-in opportunity.
- **Zero Deal Gap** — priced exactly at the return threshold; no margin of safety.
- **Negative Deal Gap** — overpriced relative to goals; negotiation or different
  terms needed.

The Deal Gap is the brand. The gap is the negotiation.

### The one-line moat

> That's where most tools stop. DealGapIQ keeps going.

### The three-act workflow

| Act | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Hunt** | Find deals three ways: Scan Property, address/zip search, Map Search — including off-market, foreclosures, pre-foreclosures, and auctions. |
| **Score** | 15-second multi-source verdict: IQ Estimate, Zillow, RentCast, Redfin, public records — plus the Deal Gap. |
| **Close** | Four Paths (price, capital, financing, income — plus a Blended Plan) + per-structure negotiation scripts and auto-filled worksheets. |

## 3. Brand Foundation

### Personality

Confident but not cold. Data-rich but plain-spoken — like a senior investor
whiteboarding a deal at a meetup. Trust-first: every number shows its source;
nothing is fabricated; missing data shows "Unavailable."

**Voice in three words:** Direct · Specific · Credible.

### The four brand pillars

1. **Numbers Over Narratives** — Strip the story. Show the math. Listing sites
   sell emotion; DealGapIQ shows whether the numbers work.
2. **Transparency Over Black Boxes** — Every assumption is visible and editable.
   Switch the data source, watch the math change.
3. **Decisions Over Browsing** — Not for scrolling listings. For making
   investment decisions — a confident offer or a confident pass.
4. **Speed Over Spreadsheets** — 45 minutes on a custom spreadsheet, or 15
   seconds on DealGapIQ. Same rigor. Automated.

### Voice — words to use

Investors **hunt** properties, **close** deals, **make** offers, **structure**
terms, **score** properties, **eliminate** bad deals fast.

Prefer: specific dollar amounts, strategy names, metric names (cap rate, DSCR,
Deal Gap, Target Buy).

### Voice — words to avoid

"Evaluate," "consider," "explore," "discover," "let us help you" — these neuter
the investor. Also avoid guru-course energy, CRM-marketing tone, generic SaaS
copy ("we help businesses grow"), and magic-wand claims.

### The manifesto

> The price tag isn't the deal. The structure is.
>
> Built for the investor who knows that good deals don't show up in a feed —
> they're constructed.
>
> *We analyze. You decide.*

### Three repeating motifs (use each ≥ once per month)

- *Stop scrolling listings. Start hunting real deals.*
- *That's where most tools stop. DealGapIQ keeps going.*
- *The price tag isn't the deal. The structure is.*

## 6. The Product in Plain Language

### 6.1 Core flow (four steps)

1. **Verdict** — One address in, multi-source valuation out. Deal Gap % and a
   plain-English explanation.
2. **Four Paths** — Three single-lever offers (price, capital, income
   re-verification) plus one Blended Plan combining smaller asks.
3. **Negotiation Playbook** — Per-structure pitch script: who to call, the
   frame, the opener, the ask, what's in it for the seller.
4. **Strategy worksheet** — Pre-loaded with the chosen path's levers, ready to
   refine across six investment strategies.

### 6.2 The three core numbers

Every analysis produces three price thresholds:

| Number | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Market Value** | What the property is worth based on comps and multi-source estimates |
| **Income Value** | Maximum price before cash flow goes negative (breakeven ceiling) |
| **Target Buy** | The price that delivers the investor's desired return |

**Deal Gap** = percentage distance between asking price and Target Buy.

**Marketing claim:** *"Knowing exactly where breakeven sits — before you even
tour a property — saves you from chasing bad deals."*

### 6.3 Six investment strategies

Every property is analyzed against all six simultaneously:

| Strategy | One-line description |
|---|---|
| **Long-Term Rental (LTR)** | Buy and hold for monthly rental income and appreciation |
| **Short-Term Rental (STR)** | Maximize income through Airbnb/VRBO |
| **BRRRR** | Buy, rehab, rent, refinance, repeat |
| **Fix & Flip** | Purchase, renovate, sell at market |
| **House Hack** | Live in one unit, rent the rest |
| **Wholesale** | Identify below-market properties and assign contracts |

### 6.4 IQ Estimate (data credibility)

Proprietary blended valuation aggregating Zillow, RentCast, Redfin, Realtor.com,
and county records. Outlier filtering when 3+ sources exist. Single source used
when only one available. **"Unavailable"** when no source has data — never
fabricated. Users can switch the active source and watch the math change.

### 6.5 Tool stack

| Tool | What it does (marketing language) |
|---|---|
| **Verdict** | 15-second investment verdict: Deal Gap, three price thresholds, deal score, plain-language guidance |
| **Strategy** | Deep-dive financial breakdown per strategy — cap rate, cash-on-cash, DSCR, full P&L |
| **DealMaker** | Interactive worksheet — adjust every assumption and see impact in real time |
| **Appraiser** | Comp-based valuation: pick comps, see adjustments, URAR-style report |
| **Estimator** | Rehab cost estimation with regional adjustments |
| **Map Search** | Investment-filtered property discovery on a map |

### 6.6 Creative finance (differentiator)

Subject-To, seller carrybacks, 0% 2nds, rate buydowns, assumable mortgages, FHA
house-hack, blended offers. **Not modeled:** wraparound mortgages and land
contracts (deferred pending state-by-state legal review).

### 6.7 Four-step workflow (SCAN → ACT)

| Step | Action | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **01 SCAN** | Enter an address or scan a property | Field-ready on your phone. Pull data the moment a property catches your eye. |
| **02 SCREEN** | Set your Buy Box, get an instant Verdict | Every property gets a PASS, MARGINAL, or BUY verdict in seconds. |
| **03 STRESS TEST** | Adjust variables in real time | Change any assumption — condition, rehab, terms — and watch the numbers shift. |
| **04 ACT** | Generate reports, track deals, close | PDFs, Excel proformas, worksheets, pipeline from first look to closing. |

### 6.8 What we deliberately don't do

- We don't give financial, legal, or investment advice. *We analyze. You decide.*
- We don't claim every property is a deal — only that **every property has more
  leverage than the asking price suggests.**
- We don't model wraparound mortgages or land contracts (pending legal review).

## 7. Audience and Targeting

### Primary persona — the active investor

- **Who:** Residential RE investor analyzing **20–100 properties to close 1–3
  per year**; owns **1–50 doors** or working toward first acquisition.
- **Experience:** Knows (or wants to know) Subject-To, seller carry, 0% 2nds.
  Has bought at least one property or is within 6 months of doing so.
- **Goal:** Find or construct deals that pencil in markets where retail listings
  rarely cash-flow. Move from scrolling to structuring offers without a weekend
  in Excel per property.
- **Core pain:** Analysis fatigue; listing sites end at price; calculators end at
  cash flow; freezes at the *pitch on the phone* step.
- **Why they buy:** Not analysis — **synthesis under time pressure**: turning a
  marginal listing into a credible offer script before someone else does.

3. Computes **Target Buy** — exact price for desired return
4. Analyzes **6 strategies simultaneously**
5. Blends **multiple data sources** into IQ Estimate with full transparency
6. Makes **every assumption editable**
7. Delivers a **verdict** — not just data, but a decision
8. Provides **four offer structures + scripts** — the synthesis layer

### What listing sites don't do

Calculate cash flow or DSCR; show rental income estimates; provide investment
strategy analysis; calculate a buy price for a target return; offer editable
financial assumptions; export lender-ready reports.

### What spreadsheets can't match

Automatic multi-source data aggregation; 15-second analysis vs. 45+ minutes;
multi-strategy analysis in one view; mobile field analysis; standardized
comparison across properties; professional PDF/Excel output.

## 15. Compliance and Guardrails

### Required framing

- **We analyze. You decide.** — use on every public-facing page and in campaign footers.
- Frame everything as **analysis**, never financial, legal, or investment advice.
- Never promise specific returns or guaranteed outcomes.

### Required disclaimers (maintain in all materials)

- "Professional use only. Not a lender."
- "Not financial advice."
- "Analytics tool, not financial advice."

### Never say

- "Every property is a deal" → say: *every property has more leverage than the asking price suggests*
- "Trusted by 10,000+ investors" (unless true and substantiable)
- Unverifiable superlatives: "the best platform," "the only tool you'll ever need"
- Wraparound mortgages or land contracts (not modeled — pending legal review)
- Specific Pro pricing until confirmed current (paid tiers TBD in this playbook)

### Competitor naming in ads

Use category names in **display ads:** "listing sites," "investor calculators."
Product names acceptable in **on-domain** comparison tables and educational content.
No red X's in comparison tables — use "—" or "partial."

### Imagery and trust

- No stock-photo "investor lifestyle" imagery.
- Product screenshots and real founder/user photos only.
- Do not fabricate testimonials or usage statistics.
