# DealGapIQ — Homepage Marketing Plan

A complete homepage rewrite designed around the Four Paths feature (three single-lever structures plus one Blended Plan). Six sections, locked copy, visual treatment notes, and an implementation brief ready for a designer or developer to build.

**Companion to:** [`docs/feature-plans/FOUR_PATHS.md`](../feature-plans/FOUR_PATHS.md) (engineering plan for Four Paths). This doc covers the positioning and copy.

---

## Strategic premise

DealGapIQ's competitive moat is the **synthesis layer** — turning a property's verdict into actionable, structure-aware offers with a negotiation script. Listing sites end at price; investor calculators end at cash flow; nobody else generates the four ways to close any gap.

The homepage's job is to communicate three things in this order:

1. **You're still hunting.** (Preserve investor identity. Don't claim every property is a deal.)
2. **When you find a candidate, we give you four ways to close it.** (The new edge.)
3. **And the script that gets you there.** (The closing tool nobody else has.)

Three repeating motif lines run through the page. Each appears at least twice:

- *"Stop scrolling listings. Start hunting real deals."* (Brand voice — preserved from existing site.)
- *"That's where most tools stop. DealGapIQ keeps going."* (The moat. Three placements.)
- *"The price tag isn't the deal. The structure is."* (The manifesto. Final-line payoff.)

## Brand voice / copy doctrine

Apply across every page, not just the homepage.

- **Hunt-language preserved.** Investors *hunt* properties. They *close* deals. They *make* offers. They *structure* terms. Use these verbs. Avoid "evaluate," "consider," "explore."
- **Specificity over abstraction.** *Subject-To* beats *"creative finance."* *$2,719 seller-carry 2nd* beats *"flexible structure."* The exact dollar number is the trust signal.
- **No magic-wand claims.** Never say or imply "every property is a deal." Say: *every property has more leverage than the asking price suggests*. Subtle but critical.
- **The investor is the hero.** The tool is the partner. Phrases like *"we'll show you,"* *"DealGapIQ writes,"* *"each path opens"* are correct. *"Let us help you discover"* is wrong (positions the tool as taking over).
- **Five-word manifesto pieces.** Reusable lines should land in 5-7 words. Easy to remember, easy to repeat, easy to screenshot.

### The three repeating motifs

1. *"Stop scrolling listings. Start hunting real deals."* — appears in the hero. Reuse on retargeting ads.
2. *"That's where most tools stop. DealGapIQ keeps going."* — appears at end of Section 2 body, repeats below the comparison table in Section 4. Two on-page placements.
3. *"The price tag isn't the deal. The structure is."* — appears as the final closing line in Section 6. Use as the manifesto in social ads, founder bio, About page.

## What this homepage deliberately does NOT do

- ❌ Pretend to have testimonials.
- ❌ Use stock photos of "investors."
- ❌ Quote influencers without permission.
- ❌ Claim every property is a deal.
- ❌ Use red Xs in the comparison table.
- ❌ Hide the methodology behind a paywall.
- ❌ Bury the CTA more than one scroll-action below the fold on mobile.

Every one of these is a temptation when traffic is low and the urge to "look bigger than you are" is strong. Resist all of them. The homepage works because it earns trust the slow way — by showing the work, naming the structures, and refusing to overclaim. Investors notice. That's what converts them.
