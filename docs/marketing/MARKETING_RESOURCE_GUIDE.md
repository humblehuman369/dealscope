## 2. Canonical Messaging

This is the single source of truth. If another doc disagrees, **this wins** — then go fix the other doc.

### 2.1 Name & category
- **Product name:** DealGapIQ (one word, capital D, G, I, Q).
- **Category we own:** *real estate deal decision intelligence* — the practical layer between listing sites (end at price) and calculators (end at cash flow).
- **The proprietary asset:** the **Deal Gap** — the % difference between the asking price and what the numbers actually support.

### 2.2 Tagline hierarchy *(three lines, three jobs — use the right one for the context)*

| Line | Job | Where to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Hunt. Score. Close.** | **Product tagline** — what it does, in 3 beats | App Store, website hero, short bios, ads, sales |
| **The price tag isn't the deal. The structure is.** | **Brand manifesto** — what we believe | Post sign-offs, founder content, emotional closes, About endings |
| **Real estate deal analysis software that shows the Deal Gap, ranks six investment strategies, and turns property numbers into investor-ready offers.** | **Search/SEO tagline** — keyword-rich | LinkedIn tagline, meta descriptions, directory listings |

> **Why three, not one:** they don't compete — they ladder. "Hunt. Score. Close." sells the *workflow*. The manifesto sells the *belief*. The SEO line wins the *search*. Lead with the product tagline everywhere a human reads first; use the SEO line where an algorithm reads first; close with the manifesto.

### 2.3 One-liners (by length)
- **Elevator (1 sentence):** Paste an address; in 15 seconds get a multi-source verdict, four ways to structure the deal, and the negotiation script for each.
- **Short bio:** DealGapIQ is the all-in-one app that helps real estate investors hunt deals, score them in 15 seconds, and close the gap between a list price and a real deal. Free to start.
- *(More bios + 10 audience-specific elevator pitches: [`DealGapIQ-Marketing-Guide.md`](./DealGapIQ-Marketing-Guide.md).)*

### 2.4 The moat (one line)
> That's where most tools stop. DealGapIQ keeps going.

The competitive edge is the **synthesis layer**: turning a verdict into structure-aware offers with a script. (Full competitive table: [`POSITIONING.md`](./POSITIONING.md) §"How we're different".)

### 2.5 The three-act story
1. **Hunt** — three ways to find deals (scan, address/zip search, map search), including off-market, foreclosures, pre-foreclosures, auctions.
2. **Score** — 15-second multi-source verdict (IQ Estimate, Zillow, RentCast, Redfin, public records) + the Deal Gap.
3. **Close** — Four Paths (price, capital, financing, income — plus a Blended Plan) + a per-structure negotiation script.

### 2.6 Proof / trust pillars
- **Founder credibility:** Brad Geisen — founder of Foreclosure.com; behind the tech powering Fannie Mae HomePath and Freddie Mac HomeSteps; 35 years in real estate data. This is the single strongest trust signal in the category.
- **Trust-first product:** every metric shows its data source; switch the source, watch the math change. Nothing is fabricated — missing data shows "Unavailable."
- **Six strategies modeled:** LTR, STR, BRRRR, Fix & Flip, House Hack, Wholesale.

### 2.7 Guardrails — what we never say
- ❌ Financial / legal / investment **advice**. Always frame as analysis. Footer line: *"We analyze. You decide."*
- ❌ "Every property is a deal." Say instead: *every property has more leverage than the asking price suggests.*
- ❌ Wraparound mortgages / land contracts (deferred pending state-by-state legal review).
- ❌ Unverifiable trust claims ("Trusted by 10,000+ investors") unless true and substantiable.
- ❌ Guru-course energy, CRM-marketing tone, generic SaaS copy ("we help businesses grow").

## 3. Audience & Personas

**Primary persona — the active investor:** analyzes 20–100 properties to close 1–3/year; owns 1–50 doors or working toward the first. Knows (or wants to know) what Subject-To, seller carry, and 0% 2nds mean. Recognizes Pace Morby.

**Core buying driver:** not analysis (they can analyze) — **synthesis under time pressure**: turning a marginal listing into a credible offer script before someone else does.

**Segments at a glance:**
| Segment | Cares most about |
|---|---|
| Active residential investor | Screening speed + the target price that makes a deal work |
| Wholesaler | Fast verdicts on off-market leads; assignment-fee math; cash-buyer directory |
| Creative finance investor | Seller-carry / blended / financing scenarios |
| BRRRR / Fix-and-Flip | Spread, financing, rent, exit; hard-money directory |
| House hacker | First-time FHA scenarios; strategy switch |
| Investor-friendly agent | Advising clients with investor-grade analysis |
| Private / hard-money lender | Borrower context, funding readiness |

### 4.2 Voice in one breath
Direct · Specific · Credible. We sound like a senior investor whiteboarding a deal at a meetup — *"Stop scrolling. Start hunting."* energy. The investor is the hero; DealGapIQ is the partner.

**Voice rules:**
- Investors **hunt** properties, **close** deals, **make** offers, **structure** terms.
- Specificity beats abstraction: *"$2,719 seller-carry 2nd at 0%"* > *"flexible structure."*
- Numbers are receipts. Lead with them.
- Avoid: "evaluate," "consider," "explore," "discover," "let us help you" — these neuter the investor.

### 4.3 Imagery rules
- **Product screenshots first** — verdict cards, four-path comparison, strategy worksheet, negotiation script. Receipts, not vibes.
- Real people only (real users, the founder). **No stock photos.**
- Charts and dollar figures over lifestyle imagery. Tight crops that read at thumbnail.
- ❌ Red X's in comparison tables (use "—" or "partial"). ❌ Clickbait arrows. ❌ Carousels that hide options on mobile.

## 6. Content System

### 6.1 Content pillars
1. **Named structures, demystified** — Sub-To, seller carry, 0% 2nds, buydowns, Morby Method, FHA house-hack. 5th-grade reading level + a real dollar example.
2. **The pitch on the phone** — what investors actually say to a seller (frame, opener, what's in it for them).
3. **Deal teardowns** — real listing → verdict → four paths → which path closes.
4. **The structure beats the price tag** — the manifesto, with examples.
5. **Show the work** — methodology; how the verdict is computed; the transparency layer.

### 6.2 Recurring series *(turn each into a branded template)*
- **Four Paths Friday** — one property, four structures, which one closes.
- **Script of the Week** — one annotated negotiation script.
- **Glossary Drop** — one creative-finance term, one diagram, one example.
- **Deal Gap Breakdown / Metric Monday** — one metric or one gap, explained.

### 6.3 Repeating motifs *(use each ≥ once/month, every channel)*
- *Stop scrolling listings. Start hunting real deals.*
- *That's where most tools stop. DealGapIQ keeps going.*
- *The price tag isn't the deal. The structure is.*

### 6.4 Value-to-promotion ratio
**4:1** — four educational/practical posts for every direct conversion post (esp. in Q1).

**Default CTAs:**

- **Secondary:** *See the Four Paths* · *Email me the Creative Finance Field Guide* (lead magnet).

**Tracking — always UTM website links:**
```
https://dealgapiq.com/?utm_source={channel}&utm_medium={type}&utm_campaign={campaign}
```
Example (LinkedIn page): `utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=company_page&utm_campaign=linkedin_launch`

**Conversion principle:** LinkedIn (and social) is a qualified *referral layer*, not a landing-page replacement. Every post has one audience, one idea, one action. Send high-intent traffic straight to Free Discovery.

## 10. Governance — keeping this library healthy

**Principles:**
1. **One source of truth per fact.** Messaging lives in §2 of this guide; visuals in the brand guides; channel tactics in channel playbooks. Don't duplicate — link.
2. **Change here first.** If a tagline, persona, or guardrail changes, edit this guide, then update downstream docs and note the change in §11.
3. **Register new assets.** Anything added to `docs/marketing/` gets a row in §1.
4. **Status discipline.** Mark each doc Canonical / Reference / Living / Artifact / Tool so readers know its authority.
5. **Quarterly review.** Refresh the competitive table, keyword tiers, and metrics targets each quarter so the page stays credible.
