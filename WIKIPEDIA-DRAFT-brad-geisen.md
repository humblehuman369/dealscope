## Why submit through AfC, not directly

- Wikipedia's notability bar (WP:GNG) requires significant coverage in **independent, reliable, secondary sources**. AfC is the path where a Wikipedia reviewer evaluates whether the cited sources clear the bar before the article goes live.
- **You (Brad) have a Conflict of Interest** under WP:COI. You can suggest the article exists and provide source material, but the article should be created by an unaffiliated editor — or submitted through AfC with full disclosure of your COI on the talk page.
- The "transitive entity link" payoff for AI visibility (ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity learning that DealGapIQ → Brad Geisen → Foreclosure.com) only triggers when this article is **published and survives notability review**. A rejected stub does nothing.
